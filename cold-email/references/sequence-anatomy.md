# Sequence anatomy — a worked 5-email cold reactivation

A real founder sequence to a cold/dormant list, no per-person data, sending one email every 2–3 days.
Product is an AI personal assistant; offer is a $30/mo Pro plan. Annotated so you can see *why* each
move is made, then adapt the skeleton to any product.

The voice is deliberately lowercase, plain-text, typed-not-composed. Notice there are no triads, no
em-dash drama, no neat bows — that's what keeps it out of the spam-feeling zone.

---

## Email 1 — the hook (day 0). No CTA on purpose.

```
Subject: what if your assistant texted you first

most ai stuff just sits there waiting for you to ask it something. which is kind of useless
honestly. a real assistant doesn't wait. it tells you "hey your 3pm moved" before you find out
the hard way.

that's the thing i've been building. it's called gaia. it actually pings you first, on whatsapp
or telegram or wherever you already are, when something needs you. you don't go to it. it comes
to you.

i'll show you what that looks like in a couple days. just wanted to put it on your radar first.

aryan
```

Why: opens a loop ("a real assistant doesn't wait"), zero ask, and explicitly promises email 2.
The absence of a link is the point — it buys permission for the rest of the sequence.

## Email 2 — value, framed around the reader (day 2–3). Reply ask + soft link.

```
Subject: the thing you redo every single morning

you probably start your day the same way every time. checking what's on, seeing what came in
overnight, working out what actually needs you today. fifteen, twenty minutes of it before
you've done anything real.

you can just hand that off. you tell gaia once, in a normal sentence, "every morning text me my
day and anything urgent from overnight" and that's it. from then on it shows up on your telegram
at 8am, done, without you lifting a finger.

that's a workflow. anything you do over and over, you describe it once and it runs on its own
forever. no setup screens, no builder, you just say it.

what's the thing you redo every day that you can't stand? reply and tell me, i'm genuinely
curious what people would hand off first. or just go build one now: heygaia.io

aryan
```

Why: opens on *their* morning, not the founder's. One concrete capability told as a mini-story.
The reply ask is the lowest-friction CTA there is, and it's a positive deliverability signal.

## Email 3 — proof via story (day 5–6). The story is the value.

```
Subject: it caught something before i did

small story. last week someone replied to a thread saying they needed an answer by end of day or
the thing was off. i was heads down and would've completely missed it.

gaia texted me. "this one looks time-sensitive, they need an answer today." pulled the email up,
i told it roughly what to say, it wrote the reply, i sent it. whole thing took a minute and i
didn't lose the deal.

that's the part people don't expect. it's not just doing tasks when you ask. it's watching the
boring stuff for you and tapping you on the shoulder when something actually matters, across your
email, your calendar, your messages, all of it.

and it does it inside whatsapp, telegram, discord, slack. not another app to check, it just lives
where you already are.

see it watch your stuff: heygaia.io/login

aryan
```

Why: the best follow-up is news/proof, not a nudge. "small story" is a human opener. CTA into the
product because by now they want to see it.

## Email 4 — the offer (day 8–9). First hard ask.

```
Subject: less than your coffee this week

ok here's the ask. gaia has a pro plan and i want to make it stupid easy to try.

it's $30 a month. that's about a week of coffee, and it's doing the work of an assistant you'd
pay a few thousand for. the math isn't really close.

what you get: it runs your workflows all day, watches your inbox and calendar and pings you
before things blow up, drafts your replies, and actually does the tasks instead of just talking
about them.

if it saves you even 20 minutes a day it's paid for itself ten times over. and if it doesn't end
up being useful, cancel it, no hard feelings, the free version isn't going anywhere.

heygaia.io/pricing

aryan
```

Why: hard ask lands at email 4, not email 1 — trust first. Concrete number, outcome math, and the
honest "don't buy it if it's not useful" qualifier that lifts trust across the whole email.

## Email 5 — recap & close (day 11–12). Channel stays open.

```
Subject: $30 vs an hour of your day, every day

quick recap, since these were spread out over a couple weeks.

gaia runs the stuff you do on repeat so you don't. it texts you first when something's urgent
instead of waiting to be asked. it clears your inbox and writes your replies. all from whatsapp
or telegram, no new app to learn.

that's the better part of an hour back, every day, for $30 a month.

if any of that sounded like your life, it's worth the thirty bucks to find out:
heygaia.io/pricing

aryan
```

Why: recap because touches were days apart. Subject reframes to the value trade, not a goodbye —
no "last email from me", so the channel survives if you send more later. No fake scarcity.

---

## Adapting this skeleton

- Keep the **shape**: hook → value → proof → offer → close, ask escalating with trust.
- Swap the **capability** in emails 2–3 for your product's single most vivid "oh, nice" moment.
- Keep emails **short** — if a touch runs longer than ~5 short paragraphs, cut.
- Always lead the sequence with whatever is genuinely *news*.
