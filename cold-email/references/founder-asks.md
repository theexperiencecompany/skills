# Founder asks — fundraising, intros, advice

Cold email where the ask isn't "buy this" but "help me" — raising an angel round, asking for an
intro, getting advice from someone you admire. Same plain-text, human, no-marketing discipline as the
rest of the skill, but the center of gravity moves. A customer asks *"does this save me time?"* An
investor asks *"will this be huge, and is this the founder to build it?"* Write to that.

## What flips for founder asks

- **Lead with traction, not the problem.** Investors see the same ten pitches a week; the only cold
  opener that reliably earns a reply is a number. "0 to [X] signups, growing [Y]% a week, no ad
  spend." No traction yet? Lead with an unfair insight or absurd build velocity instead.
- **Sound like a founder, not a campaign.** No logo, no button, no unsubscribe footer — these are 1:1
  emails to a hand-picked list. The moment it looks designed, it reads as a blast and the personal
  weight is gone. (Legal note: true 1:1 mail to individuals doesn't need an unsubscribe the way a bulk
  campaign does — but the second you send to a *list* through a platform, add one.)
- **Ask for help, not money.** The email's job is a 20-minute conversation, never a check. "could i
  grab 20 minutes to show you the product live?" Asking for the wire in email one reads as naive.
- **Make it about them.** One true line on why *this* person — a company they built, a founder they
  backed, an essay they wrote. An angel list is dozens of people, not thousands, so real
  personalization is actually doable and it's the whole game.
- **The warm intro beats cold every time.** If there's a mutual connection, the highest-leverage move
  isn't sending the cold email — it's writing the short, forwardable blurb the connector pastes.
- **Honest scarcity only.** "raising [$X], about [N]% committed, closing in [N] weeks" works — but
  investors have the sharpest BS detectors alive. One inflated number ends it.
- **Follow up with news, not a nudge.** "just checking in" is dead weight. Wait until there's a real
  milestone, then the follow-up writes itself.

## The 3-email raise thread

A founder-voice angel sequence, sent ~4–6 days apart to a small, researched list. Bracketed `[..]`
placeholders are filled with real numbers per recipient. Lowercase, plain, no footer.

### Email 1 — the intro

```
Subject: going all in on gaia — would value your read

hey [first name],

i'm aryan, one of the founders of gaia. quick and honest email.

i just quit my job to build gaia full-time. it's a personal assistant that actually does the work
— runs your inbox, handles your calendar, automates the repetitive stuff, and messages you on
whatsapp or telegram before things slip through.

we're at [X signups] and growing [Y]% a week with basically no marketing spend. that's the number
that made me jump.

i'm putting together a small angel round to hire a couple of engineers and give this real runway.
i'm reaching out to you specifically because [one true line about them], and i'd genuinely value
having you involved.

could i grab 20 minutes to show you the product live? no deck to sit through, i'll just walk you
through what we've built and where it's going.

either way, thanks for reading.

aryan
```

Why: traction in line three, "you specifically because [real reason]," and the ask is 20 minutes,
not a check. "either way, thanks for reading" lowers the pressure and reads human.

### Email 2 — the follow-up (news, not a nudge)

```
Subject: a real gaia update, not a nudge

hey [first name],

didn't want to hit you with a "just following up", so i waited until there was something real to
share.

since i last wrote: [new milestone — crossed [X] users / shipped [Y] / signed [Z]]. [one line on
why it matters].

still putting the angel round together, and i'd still love to have you in it. same easy ask as
before — 20 minutes, i show you the product live, you tell me what you actually think.

want me to send over a couple of times?

aryan
```

Why: names the anti-pattern out loud ("didn't want to hit you with a just following up") and earns
the re-open with a real milestone. The ask is unchanged and still easy.

### Email 3 — the graceful close

```
Subject: last note from me on this

hey [first name],

last one from me on this, promise.

i know inboxes are brutal and timing is everything with this stuff. if it's not a fit right now, i
completely understand — no hard feelings at all.

two small asks if you have a second. a one-line "not for me because ___" would genuinely help me
get sharper at this. and if anyone comes to mind who'd be excited about what we're building, an
intro would mean a lot.

either way, thank you for the time. i'll keep building regardless.

aryan
```

Why: closes the loop with grace, and converts a non-answer into two low-friction asks — feedback
and an intro. "i'll keep building regardless" signals conviction without neediness. Note: unlike a
customer sequence, a definitive "last one" is good etiquette here — you're closing *this thread*,
not the relationship.

## The forwardable warm-intro blurb

When a mutual connection offers to intro you, send them this — 4 lines they can paste with one
sentence of their own on top. Make it effortless to forward.

```
[Founder name] is building [one line: what it is + the single most impressive number]. They're
raising a small angel round and I thought of you because [why this investor]. Worth a quick look —
here's [the product / a 2-min loom]. Want me to connect you?
```

## Adapting

- Swap the **traction line** for your single most credible number; if you have none yet, swap in the
  insight or the velocity.
- Keep the **shape**: intro (traction + why them + 20-min ask) → news follow-up → graceful close.
- Same ask works for advice and intros, not just money — change "angel round" to "15 minutes of your
  brain" or "an intro to [person]".
